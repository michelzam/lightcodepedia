# 🏆 The last chapter is yours

Every story you've read so far was about **Lucky** 🐕 — one dog, one rescue, one
small app that helped. You learned by saving *him*.

But this is a **never-ending story**[^never-ending]. The point was never Lucky.
The point was *you* — and the moment you'd pick up the pen and write a chapter of
your own. This is that moment.

> Like the book where the reader is pulled inside and asked to name the new
> world — the course doesn't end, it *hands off*. Every learner's capstone is a
> new beginning someone else might one day build on.
{: .speaker-note }

## 🌍 Choose a world to save

You don't need a big world. You need **one real person with one real problem** —
the same shape as Lucky's. Start there and the app almost designs itself.

```yaml
who: "Someone you actually know"
their_problem: "A specific moment when they're stuck"
the_rescue: "What your app does the instant they open it"
why_you: "Why this one matters to you"
```
{: .form editable="true" #capstone_frame }

That little card is your **problem statement**[^problem-statement] — the seed of
the whole thing. Keep it to one person; you can save the world one rescue at a
time.

## 🧭 Write it the way you learned

You already know the moves — you made them all on Lucky. Now run the same loop,
but *you* hold the pen:

1. 🐾 **Discover** — use a finished version in your head first. What does the
   happy ending look like? (Behavior before architecture.)
2. 🔧 **Design** — the screen, the model, the code: three views of your one idea.
3. 📜 **Specs** — write the promises your app must keep, and let them be checked.
4. 🚢 **Ship it** — your own fork, your own key, your own deploy.

Pull whatever you need from the [catalog](/micro_build_ai/catalog) as the need
arises — exactly the just-in-time habit you've been practising. Aim for an
**MVP**[^mvp]: the smallest version that genuinely rescues your one person.

## 📖 Capstone, decoded — the real words

The story called it "saving a world." Here's what you're actually producing:

| In the story | What professionals call it |
|---|---|

[^never-ending]: **Never-ending story** — a course shaped so the final chapter is written by the learner; each capstone becomes a new starting point rather than an end.
[^problem-statement]: **Problem statement** — one or two sentences naming exactly who has the problem, what the problem is, and when it bites. The seed every app grows from.
[^mvp]: **Minimum viable product (MVP)** — the smallest version of an app that delivers real value to one user, so you can learn from a working thing instead of a plan.
[^persona]: **User persona** — a concrete stand-in for your target user (a specific person, not "everyone"), used to keep design decisions grounded.
[^user-story]: **User story** — a one-line promise in the form *"As a … I want … so that …"*, describing one thing the user can do and why it matters.
[^capstone]: **Capstone** — a final, self-directed project that puts everything learned to work on a problem the learner chooses.
